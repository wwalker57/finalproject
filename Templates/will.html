My entire life, I have been surrounded by computers and technology. I have always been my family
and neighborhood tech guy-- repairing and fixing broken computers. Somewhere in my mind, I always
knew that I wanted to do something that involved computers and computer science is just the thing.

To high school students who don't know what to study: keep it simple during the first years of
college. It's perfectly okay to not know what you want to do, but it's also important that you
explore a little bit of everything your college offers to find your niche. Don't just take general
education requirements; explore some elective courses that seem interesting -- join some extra
curriculars and take part in campus activities. There's something somewhere for you!

More advice: Stop worrying about the things you can't control! You've gone through 13 years
of school to reach this point, and you are ready. My advice to graduating seniors is to stay strong
and stay positive. Finish high school strong, with the best GPA possible and apply for as many scholarships as you can.
